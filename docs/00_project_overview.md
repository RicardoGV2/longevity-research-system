# Project Overview

## Purpose

This project builds a structured health and longevity research system.

It is designed to support:

- Personal baseline creation.
- Medical-style intake.
- Measurement planning.
- Biomarker tracking.
- Nutrition and recipe experimentation.
- Exercise and physical-load analysis.
- Sleep and recovery monitoring.
- Environmental exposure tracking.
- Evidence review and research backlog management.

## Fixed objective

The objective is fixed:

> Extend healthspan and functional longevity by identifying, measuring, and improving variables that affect long-term health, resilience, autonomy, performance, and disease risk.

## What this is not at first

This is not initially:

- A gym routine.
- A diet plan.
- A meal plan.
- A weight-loss-only system.
- A program limited by current access to gym, equipment, schedule, or preferences.

Those practical constraints are implementation variables and come later.

## Current working model

1. Define the ideal scientific model.
2. Identify relevant body systems.
3. Define measurable variables.
4. Collect baseline data.
5. Classify measurements by usefulness and feasibility.
6. Generate hypotheses.
7. Design experiments.
8. Observe results.
9. Adjust based on evidence.
10. Adapt to real-world constraints after the model is clear.
